
# Marine Propulsion & Auxiliary Systems – P&ID (Plant 3D)

##  Overview

This project presents a simplified **Piping & Instrumentation Diagram (P&ID)** of a marine propulsion system developed using Autodesk Plant 3D.

The diagram illustrates how the **main engine (E-101)** is supported by essential auxiliary systems required for safe and efficient operation, including:

* Fuel Oil System
* Jacket Water (Fresh Water) Cooling System
* Sea Water Cooling System
* Lubricating Oil System

The goal of this project was to demonstrate **system-level engineering understanding**, combining mechanical, fluid, and control aspects into a clear and structured diagram.

---

##  System Description

###  Fuel Oil System

The fuel system supplies conditioned fuel to the engine:

* Storage tanks (TK-201, TK-202)
* Transfer and booster pumps (P-201, P-202)
* Filtration and heating stages

**Function:**
Ensures clean, pressurized fuel is delivered to the engine for combustion.

---

###  Jacket Water Cooling System

Closed-loop cooling system responsible for engine temperature control:

* Hot coolant exits engine
* Temperature control valve regulates flow
* Heat exchanger removes heat
* Circulation pump returns cooled water to engine

**Function:**
Maintains optimal engine operating temperature and prevents overheating.

---

###  Sea Water Cooling System

Open-loop system used for heat rejection:

* Seawater intake → strainer → pump
* Heat exchanger (cooling jacket water)
* Discharge overboard

**Function:**
Removes heat from the closed cooling loop and transfers it to the environment.

---

###  Lubricating Oil System

Ensures proper lubrication of engine components:

* Oil tank and pump (P-301)
* Filtration unit
* Oil cooler

**Function:**
Reduces friction, removes heat, and protects internal engine components.

---

##  Engineering Concepts Demonstrated

* System decomposition into functional subsystems
* Flow direction and process logic
* Equipment tagging (E-101, P-XXX, TK-XXX, etc.)
* Separation of fluid systems and mechanical energy flow
* Basic instrumentation (pressure, temperature, level)
* Understanding of engine support systems and interdependencies

##  Project Scope

This is a **concept-level P&ID**, intended for:

* Learning and portfolio demonstration
* System understanding and visualization

It does **not represent a class-approved or production-ready design**.

---

##  Key Insight

The **main engine (E-101)** acts as the central hub of the system:

* Fuel system → provides energy
* Cooling systems → remove excess heat
* Lubrication system → reduces wear and friction

All auxiliary systems interact **through the engine**, but remain functionally independent.
